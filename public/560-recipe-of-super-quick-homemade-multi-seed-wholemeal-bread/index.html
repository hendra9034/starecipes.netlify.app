The ingredients needed to make Multi-seed wholemeal bread:
  1. Prepare strong wholemeal bread flour
  2. Prepare and 1/2 tablespoons dried yeast
  3. Prepare mixed seeds
  4. Prepare oil
  5. Take runny honey
  6. Prepare and 1/2 teaspoons salt
  7. Prepare and 1/2 cups warm water

Instructions to make Multi-seed wholemeal bread:
  1. Ingredients
  2. Add the yeast, 2 cups of flour and the water to a bowl and mix.
  3. Leave for 15-20 minutes until it forms a bubbly sponge.
  4. While that is working grease and dust two bread tins. I used 9x5 inch tins but for a greater rise you may want to use 8.5x4.5 inch ones.
  5. The reason for dusting the tins is that I’ve found that sometimes the loaves can stick to the tins. Dusting solves that issue.
  6. Add the rest of the ingredients to the bowl and mix using the K beater.
  7. Tip: if you use the same measure for oil and honey, measure the oil first and then the honey. The honey will not stick to the measure.
  8. When all the ingredients are mixed swap the mixer to be the dough hook. Knead for 6-7 minutes. If you do not have a dough hook manually knead for 10 minutes.
  9. Once the dough is clearing the bowl empty out onto a lean surface. Form into a round and the cut into two.
  10. For each half. Spread the dough out to be slightly longer and wider than the loaf tins. Then fold and pinch together.
  11. Stretch the ends and fold into the middle. Turn over and place into a tin.
  12. Leave in a warm place for an hour or until the dough is 1/2-1 inch over the top of the tins.
  13. After about 50 minutes preheat the oven. 350F/180C/gas 4
  14. When the dough has finished rising place into the center of the oven and cook for 30 minutes, turning after 20.
  15. Turn the bread out and place on a rack to cool.
  16. Leave for at least an hour before cutting the fresh loaves.
  17. Enjoy.
